Amazon Brazil Operations Analysis

Strategic Insights for Amazon India

This case study analyzes Amazon Brazil's e-commerce operations to identify transferable insights for Amazon India. The analysis focuses on payment behavior, customer segmentation, revenue trends, product pricing, seasonal demand, and loyalty opportunities using SQL and business intelligence methods.

View SQL File Download Case Study

Business Context

Amazon operates across multiple international markets, where customer behavior, payment preferences, seasonal demand, and category performance can vary significantly. This project uses Amazon Brazil operational data as a benchmark to uncover patterns that can inform strategic recommendations for Amazon India’s e-commerce growth.

Business Objective

  • Analyze Amazon Brazil's operational and transactional data.
  • Identify customer payment preferences and revenue drivers.
  • Study seasonal and monthly sales trends.
  • Segment customers based on purchase frequency and order value.
  • Translate findings into practical recommendations for Amazon India.

Dataset Overview

Data Scope

E-commerce transactional data covering customers, orders, payments, products, order items, and category-level information.

Analysis Type

SQL-based business analysis covering revenue, payments, pricing, customer behavior, segmentation, and operational trends.

Business Use Case

Use Brazil market behavior to generate strategic insights that can support Amazon India's marketing, payment, inventory, and retention decisions.

Methodology

  • Used SQL joins to connect orders, customers, products, payments, and order items.
  • Applied aggregations to calculate revenue, order counts, average values, and category performance.
  • Used CASE statements to classify customers and order value segments.
  • Used CTEs and subqueries for loyalty segmentation, seasonal analysis, and high-performing product analysis.
  • Used window functions to rank high-value customers and analyze month-over-month payment growth.
  • Exported selected outputs into Excel to support visual trend analysis.

Key Analyses Performed

Payment Analysis

Evaluated payment type distribution, average transaction values, and monthly payment growth.

Revenue Analysis

Analyzed monthly revenue, seasonal sales patterns, and high-performing revenue periods.

Customer Segmentation

Classified customers into occasional, regular, loyal, returning, and high-value customer groups.

Product & Pricing Analysis

Studied price variation, top revenue categories, above-average selling products, and catalogue quality issues.

Key Insights

Credit Cards Were the Dominant Payment Method

Credit cards accounted for 73.9% of total orders, showing strong preference for card-based purchases and indicating an opportunity to promote EMI, cashback, and bank-partner offers.

Higher Average Payment Value via Credit Cards

Credit cards had the highest average payment value, suggesting customers are more comfortable using credit cards for higher-value transactions.

Seasonal Demand Patterns Were Visible

Monthly and seasonal revenue analysis showed clear demand fluctuations, which can support better campaign timing and inventory planning.

Customer Loyalty Opportunities Exist

Customer segmentation revealed large occasional customer groups and smaller loyal groups, creating opportunities for repeat-purchase campaigns and tier-based loyalty programs.

High-Value Customers Can Be Targeted

Customers were ranked by average order value to identify premium buyers suitable for exclusive rewards, priority support, and personalized recommendations.

Product Categories Showed Pricing Gaps

Wide price variation across categories indicates opportunities for premium positioning, bundling, price filters, and segmented promotions.

Recommendations for Amazon India

  • Strengthen credit card partnerships: Launch bank offers, cashback programs, and EMI options to drive higher-value purchases.
  • Build loyalty journeys: Convert occasional customers into regular buyers using personalized follow-ups and repeat-purchase incentives.
  • Plan seasonal campaigns: Use monthly and seasonal trends to prepare inventory, discounts, and marketing before peak periods.
  • Target high-value customers: Create VIP rewards, early access deals, and premium support for high AOV customer segments.
  • Improve category-level pricing: Use category price variation to design budget, mid-range, and premium product strategies.
  • Improve catalogue data quality: Clean missing or incomplete category names to improve search, recommendations, and reporting accuracy.

Business Impact

Revenue Growth

Identifies payment and category strategies that can increase average order value and sales volume.

Customer Retention

Supports loyalty and repeat-purchase strategies through customer segmentation.

Operational Planning

Improves campaign timing, inventory planning, and product visibility using demand trends.
