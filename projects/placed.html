Placed Candidate Employability Analysis

Identifying Drivers of Placement Success

This project analyzes student placement outcomes to identify the strongest drivers of employability. The analysis evaluates academic performance, technical skills, practical exposure, professional competencies, and candidate archetypes to help Placed improve placement success and recruiter decision-making.

View Tableau Dashboard View Python Notebook Download Case Study

Business Context

Placed is a hiring platform that connects students with recruiters. The business goal is to improve placement success by identifying which candidate attributes contribute most to successful placement outcomes. The project focuses on understanding why some students with strong academics fail to secure jobs while others with moderate academics achieve successful placements.

North Star Metric

Placement Success Rate: 50.02%

Placement Success Rate measures the percentage of students who successfully get placed. This metric helps evaluate the effectiveness of candidate preparation, recruiter matching, and employability improvement initiatives.

Business Objective

  • Identify the strongest factors influencing student placement success.
  • Compare placed and non-placed candidates across academic, technical, and professional dimensions.
  • Evaluate whether academics alone are sufficient to explain placement outcomes.
  • Develop candidate archetypes to support recruiter decision-making.
  • Create an interactive Tableau dashboard for stakeholders.

Analysis Approach

Data Quality Check

Validated missing values, duplicates, consistency, and outlier behavior before analysis.

EDA & Driver Analysis

Analyzed technical skills, academics, internships, projects, communication, and problem-solving factors.

Candidate Archetypes

Grouped students into employability profiles based on skill strength and placement readiness.

Dashboarding

Built a Tableau dashboard to help stakeholders monitor placement performance and candidate readiness.

Key Insights

Technical Skills Were Stronger Than Academics Alone

Students with stronger technical skills achieved better placement outcomes, even when academic performance was only moderate.

Practical Exposure Improved Employability

Internships, projects, certifications, and hands-on experience increased recruiter confidence and improved placement probability.

Professional Skills Also Mattered

Communication, teamwork, leadership, and problem-solving skills supported workplace readiness and improved employability outcomes.

Balanced Profiles Performed Best

Candidates who combined technical capability, practical exposure, and professional readiness achieved the highest placement success.

Candidate Archetypes

Elite Employability Profiles

Placement Rate: 82% – 87%

Strong across academics, technical skills, internships, projects, communication, and problem-solving.

Technical-Core Achievers

Placement Rate: 70% – 83%

Strong technical capability with good project exposure, even when academics are moderate.

Industry-Ready Practitioners

Placement Rate: 55% – 77%

Strong practical exposure and professional readiness with room to improve technical depth.

High-Risk Employability Profiles

Placement Rate: 15% – 25%

Weak technical and practical foundations requiring targeted development support.

Tableau Dashboard

The dashboard provides a centralized view of placement success, candidate performance, skill drivers, segmentation, and stakeholder-level insights.

Recommendations

For Placed

  • Create an Employability Score combining technical, practical, and professional competencies.
  • Use candidate archetypes to identify high-potential and at-risk students.
  • Prioritize skill-based shortlisting over academic-only filters.

For Students

  • Build strong technical foundations through coding and domain skills.
  • Increase practical exposure through internships, projects, hackathons, and certifications.
  • Strengthen communication, teamwork, and problem-solving abilities.

For Recruiters

  • Use multidimensional evaluation instead of relying only on academics.
  • Consider Technical-Core Achievers and Industry-Ready Practitioners.
  • Use employability segmentation to improve candidate selection efficiency.
