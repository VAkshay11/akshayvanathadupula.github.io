Flipkart Customer Support Analytics

Customer Satisfaction, SLA, Channel & Service Performance Analysis

This project analyzes Flipkart-style customer support interactions to understand what drives customer satisfaction. The analysis evaluates CSAT, SLA performance, support channels, call duration, city-level performance, and service bottlenecks using Excel dashboards and pivot-based analysis.

Business Context

Customer support quality directly affects customer trust, retention, and brand perception. For an e-commerce platform, understanding how service channels, SLA adherence, call duration, and regional support performance influence CSAT helps improve operational efficiency and customer experience.

Business Objective

  • Analyze customer satisfaction across support channels.
  • Evaluate whether SLA performance strongly impacts CSAT.
  • Compare call centers and cities based on CSAT and call duration.
  • Identify support channels that require improvement.
  • Provide recommendations to improve customer experience and service efficiency.

Dataset Overview

Data Scope

30,000 customer support records covering CSAT scores, sentiment, call duration, SLA status, city, state, channel, and call center.

Support Channels

Analyzed customer interactions across Call-Center, Chatbot, Email, and Web support channels.

Dashboarding

Built Excel pivot tables and dashboard views to compare CSAT, SLA, channel, location, and duration patterns.

Analysis Approach

SLA vs CSAT

Compared customer satisfaction across Above SLA, Below SLA, and Within SLA categories.

Channel Performance

Evaluated CSAT and average call duration across Call-Center, Chatbot, Email, and Web channels.

Location Analysis

Compared call centers and cities to identify regional service performance differences.

Duration Bucketing

Grouped calls into short, medium, and long duration buckets to evaluate their relationship with CSAT.

Key Insights

SLA Alone Did Not Strongly Explain CSAT

Average CSAT was very close across SLA categories: Above SLA, Below SLA, and Within SLA all stayed around the mid-5 range. This suggests SLA status alone may not be the strongest driver of customer satisfaction.

Channel Had a Stronger Impact Than SLA

Channel-level analysis showed clearer differences in customer satisfaction. Web and Call-Center interactions performed better, while Chatbot had comparatively lower CSAT.

Call-Center and Web Performed Better

Call-Center and Web support showed stronger CSAT performance, indicating that customers may prefer channels where issue resolution feels more direct or reliable.

Chatbot Needs Improvement

Chatbot interactions showed weaker CSAT compared to other channels, suggesting improvement opportunities in escalation, response quality, and self-service experience.

City-Level Performance Varied

Call center location analysis showed that Chennai had stronger average CSAT, while some locations such as Kolkata showed comparatively lower satisfaction.

Duration Was Not the Only Driver

Average call duration remained close across channels, meaning longer or shorter calls alone do not fully explain customer satisfaction. Resolution quality likely matters more.

Business Recommendations

  • Improve chatbot resolution quality: Add better escalation paths, smarter intent detection, and post-chat feedback loops.
  • Use channel-specific strategies: Treat Web, Call-Center, Email, and Chatbot separately instead of using one support strategy for all channels.
  • Study high-performing locations: Analyze practices from stronger-performing centers such as Chennai and apply learnings to lower-performing locations.
  • Move beyond SLA-only tracking: Combine SLA with resolution quality, sentiment, repeat contact rate, and customer feedback.
  • Optimize support routing: Route complex or sensitive issues to higher-performing support channels instead of relying heavily on automation.
  • Monitor CSAT by channel and city: Build recurring dashboards to track satisfaction trends and operational improvement areas.

Business Impact

Better Customer Experience

Identifies service channels and regions where customer experience can be improved.

Smarter Support Operations

Helps prioritize improvements across chatbot, call-center, web, and email support.

Improved CSAT Monitoring

Supports ongoing dashboard-based tracking of satisfaction, SLA, duration, and channel performance.
